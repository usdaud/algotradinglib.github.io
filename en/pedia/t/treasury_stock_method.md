# Treasury Stock Method

The Treasury Stock Method (TSM) is a widely used [accounting](../a/accounting.md) technique for calculating the potential [dilution](../d/dilution.md) of [earnings](../e/earnings.md) per share (EPS) that could occur if the outstanding convertible securities like [options](../o/options.md) and [warrants](../w/warrants_in_trading.md) were exercised. In [finance](../f/finance.md), this method assumes that the proceeds a company receives from the [exercise](../e/exercise.md) of [options](../o/options.md) and [warrants](../w/warrants_in_trading.md) are used entirely to buy back [shares](../s/shares.md) at the average [market price](../m/market_price.md) during the reporting period.

## Overview

The Treasury Stock Method is integral to calculating diluted EPS, providing a more conservative estimation than basic EPS. The goal is to give investors a clearer picture of the [earnings](../e/earnings.md) attributable to each share, considering the potential [dilution](../d/dilution.md) from existing convertible instruments.

## Key Concepts

### Convertible Instruments
Convertible instruments are securities that can be converted into [common stock](../c/common_stock.md). Examples include stock [options](../o/options.md), [warrants](../w/warrants_in_trading.md), and convertible bonds. These instruments can potentially dilute the [shares](../s/shares.md) outstanding when exercised, increasing the total share count and thereby diluting EPS.

### Diluted Earnings Per Share (EPS)
Diluted EPS provides a "worst-case" scenario by considering the impact of all convertible instruments. It's calculated by adding the [shares](../s/shares.md) issuable from these instruments to the existing share count, then adjusting net [income](../i/income.md) for any associated costs (e.g., [interest](../i/interest.md) on convertible [debt](../d/debt.md)).

### Basic EPS vs. Diluted EPS
- **Basic EPS**: Calculated by dividing net [income](../i/income.md) by the [weighted average](../w/weighted_average.md) [shares](../s/shares.md) outstanding during the period.
- **Diluted EPS**: Accounts for all potential dilutive securities, providing a more diluted share count and thus a lower EPS if these securities were converted.

## Calculation Steps

1. **Identify Convertible Instruments**: Determine all [options](../o/options.md), [warrants](../w/warrants_in_trading.md), and other convertible securities outstanding at the end of the reporting period.
2. **Calculate Proceeds**: Multiply the number of in-the-[money](../m/money.md) convertible securities by their [exercise price](../e/excersise_price.md) to find the proceeds received upon [exercise](../e/exercise.md).
3. **Repurchase [Shares](../s/shares.md)**: Divide the total proceeds by the average [market price](../m/market_price.md) of the [shares](../s/shares.md) during the reporting period to calculate the number of [shares](../s/shares.md) that can be repurchased.
4. **Calculate Net Increase in [Shares](../s/shares.md)**: Subtract the [shares](../s/shares.md) repurchased from the total new [shares](../s/shares.md) issued due to the [exercise](../e/exercise.md) of [options](../o/options.md) and [warrants](../w/warrants_in_trading.md).
5. **Compute Diluted EPS**: Add the net increase in [shares](../s/shares.md) to the outstanding [shares](../s/shares.md) to calculate diluted EPS.

### Example Calculation

Consider a company with the following:
- 1,000,000 [shares](../s/shares.md) outstanding.
- 100,000 stock [options](../o/options.md) with an [exercise price](../e/excersise_price.md) of $20.
- Average [market price](../m/market_price.md) during the period is $25.
- Net [income](../i/income.md) is $5,000,000.

**Step-by-Step Calculation**:

1. **Proceeds from [Exercise](../e/exercise.md)**: 100,000 [options](../o/options.md) * $20 ([exercise price](../e/excersise_price.md)) = $2,000,000
2. **[Shares](../s/shares.md) Repurchased**: $2,000,000 / $25 ([market price](../m/market_price.md)) = 80,000 [shares](../s/shares.md)
3. **Net Increase in [Shares](../s/shares.md)**: 100,000 [options](../o/options.md) - 80,000 [shares](../s/shares.md) repurchased = 20,000 [shares](../s/shares.md)
4. **New Diluted Share Count**: 1,000,000 [shares](../s/shares.md) + 20,000 = 1,020,000 [shares](../s/shares.md)
5. **Diluted EPS**: $5,000,000 / 1,020,000 = $4.90

In this example:
- **Basic EPS**: $5,000,000 / 1,000,000 = $5.00
- **Diluted EPS**: $5,000,000 / 1,020,000 = $4.90

## Applications in Financial Analysis

The application of TSM extends beyond the pure calculation of EPS. It is also used in:
- **[Valuation Models](../v/valuation_models.md)**: Adjusting projected EPS in discounted [cash flow](../c/cash_flow.md) (DCF) analyses.
- **Financial Reporting**: Complying with [accounting](../a/accounting.md) standards like US GAAP and IFRS.
- **Investment Decision-Making**: Assessing the impact of [equity compensation](../e/equity_compensation.md) plans, [convertible bond](../c/convertible_bond.md) issuance, and other [dilution](../d/dilution.md)-related scenarios.

### Companies Utilizing TSM

Several major corporations and financial firms use the Treasury Stock Method in their financial reports. For example:
- Apple Inc. incorporates TSM in their quarterly and annual reports to reflect the potential [dilution](../d/dilution.md) from employee stock [options](../o/options.md).
- Goldman Sachs uses TSM for accurate financial disclosures and [investor](../i/investor.md) communications.

## Advanced Considerations

### Complex Capital Structures
For companies with complex [capital](../c/capital.md) structures, the TSM must adapt to [multiple](../m/multiple.md) levels of convertible instruments, each influencing the overall diluted share count differently. This complexity requires detailed tracking and precise calculations.

### Anti-Dilutive Securities
Securities that would increase EPS if exercised are considered anti-dilutive and are not included in the diluted EPS calculation. Properly identifying and excluding these instruments is necessary to avoid misrepresentation.

### Sensitivity Analysis
Evaluating the sensitivity of diluted EPS to changes in the [exercise price](../e/excersise_price.md), [market price](../m/market_price.md), and other variables can provide additional insights into the potential impact of convertible securities on a company's [earnings](../e/earnings.md).

## Conclusion

The Treasury Stock Method plays a pivotal role in modern [financial analysis](../f/financial_analysis.md) and reporting. It ensures that all potential sources of [dilution](../d/dilution.md) are considered, providing a more comprehensive and conservative estimate of [earnings](../e/earnings.md) per share. This method enhances [transparency](../t/transparency.md), supports informed investment decisions, and aligns with regulatory standards, making it essential for companies, analysts, and investors alike.
