# United States Natural Gas Fund (UNG)

The United States Natural Gas [Fund](../f/fund.md) (UNG) is one of the most prominent [exchange](../e/exchange.md)-traded funds (ETFs) that focus specifically on the natural gas [market](../m/market.md). This [fund](../f/fund.md) was created to track the price changes of natural gas, using [futures contracts](../f/futures_contracts.md) as its primary [investment vehicle](../i/investment_vehicle.md).

## Overview

### Purpose and Objective
The primary objective of the United States Natural Gas [Fund](../f/fund.md) is to provide investors with a simple and cost-effective method of gaining exposure to the price movements of natural gas. UNG aims to reflect the daily changes in percentage terms of the [spot price](../s/spot_price.md) of natural gas delivered at the [Henry Hub](../h/henry_hub.md), Louisiana. This is achieved through the rolling over of [futures contracts](../f/futures_contracts.md) on natural gas, a process involving buying near-month contracts and selling them before they expire to maintain constant exposure to the [commodity](../c/commodity.md).

### Structure and Management
As an ETF, UNG provides [liquidity](../l/liquidity.md) and ease of access for individual and institutional investors alike. It is managed by United States [Commodity](../c/commodity.md) Funds LLC (USCF), a company specializing in creating [commodity](../c/commodity.md)-based ETFs. The [fund](../f/fund.md)'s governance and daily operations are executed in compliance with regulatory standards to ensure [transparency](../t/transparency.md) and [investor](../i/investor.md) protection.

## Investment Strategy

### Futures Contracts
Investment in the United States Natural Gas [Fund](../f/fund.md) primarily involves [futures contracts](../f/futures_contracts.md) on the NYMEX (New York Mercantile [Exchange](../e/exchange.md)). [Futures contracts](../f/futures_contracts.md) are agreements to buy or sell a predetermined amount of natural gas at a specified price on a future date. UNG invests in these contracts to generate returns that mirror the price movements of natural gas.

### Contract Rolling
UNG follows a rolling strategy where contracts are sold before they expire, and new contracts with longer maturities are purchased. This rolling process is critical to maintaining continuous exposure to the [underlying](../u/underlying.md) [commodity](../c/commodity.md) while avoiding the [physical delivery](../p/physical_delivery_in_trading.md) of natural gas.

### Contango and Backwardation
UNG's performance is influenced by [market](../m/market.md) conditions like [contango](../c/contango.md) and [backwardation](../b/backwardation.md). [Contango](../c/contango.md) occurs when future prices are higher than spot prices, leading to potential losses or reduced gains as the [fund](../f/fund.md) rolls over contracts. [Backwardation](../b/backwardation.md), on the other hand, happens when future prices are lower than current prices, potentially benefiting the [fund](../f/fund.md) during rollovers.

## Performance and Risks

### Historical Performance
UNG's performance has varied over the years, reflecting the [volatility](../v/volatility.md) and fluctuations inherent in the natural gas [market](../m/market.md). Its returns can be particularly influenced by seasonal [demand](../d/demand.md), geopolitical events, and changes in natural gas production and [supply](../s/supply.md).

### Market Volatility
As with any investment in commodities, UNG is subject to significant [market](../m/market.md) [volatility](../v/volatility.md). Natural gas prices can be highly unpredictable, affected by factors such as weather patterns, technological advancements in extraction methods, and regulatory changes.

### Counterparty Risk
Since the [fund](../f/fund.md) relies heavily on [futures contracts](../f/futures_contracts.md), there's a need to consider [counterparty risk](../c/counterparty_risk.md), which occurs if the entity on the other side of the contract fails to fulfill its [obligations](../o/obligation.md). UNG takes measures to mitigate this [risk](../r/risk.md) by diversifying its exposure across [multiple](../m/multiple.md) counterparties.

## Pros and Cons

### Pros
1. **Direct Exposure**: Provides direct exposure to natural gas prices without the need to deal with the complexities of [futures](../f/futures.md) trading.
2. **[Liquidity](../l/liquidity.md)**: As an ETF, UNG offers [liquidity](../l/liquidity.md), enabling investors to easily buy and sell [shares](../s/shares.md) on the stock [exchange](../e/exchange.md).
3. **[Diversification](../d/diversification.md)**: Can be a useful tool for diversifying a broader investment portfolio.

### Cons
1. **High [Volatility](../v/volatility.md)**: Natural gas prices can be extremely volatile, which can lead to significant fluctuations in the [fund](../f/fund.md)'s [value](../v/value.md).
2. **[Contango](../c/contango.md) Risks**: The rolling of [futures contracts](../f/futures_contracts.md) in a [contango](../c/contango.md) [market](../m/market.md) can result in losses over time.
3. **Management Fees**: Incur management fees, which can eat into the returns over the long term.

## Tax Considerations

Investors in UNG should be aware of the tax implications associated with [investing](../i/investing.md) in an ETF focused on commodities. Gains from such investments are subject to different tax treatments compared to traditional [stocks](../s/stock.md) and bonds, and investors may face a mix of short-term and long-term [capital](../c/capital.md) gains [taxes](../t/taxes.md). It is advisable to consult a tax professional to fully understand the tax [obligations](../o/obligation.md).
