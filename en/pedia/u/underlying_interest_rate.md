# Underlying Interest Rate

The underlying interest rate is the reference rate used to price interest rate derivatives or related instruments. It is the base rate on which cash flows and discounting are calculated.

## Examples
- Overnight rates used for swaps.
- Term benchmark rates used for loans and futures.
- Central bank policy rates used as reference levels.

## Why it matters
Changes in the underlying rate affect the valuation of rate sensitive instruments such as swaps, futures, and bonds. Traders monitor both the level and expectations of future changes.

## Example
A swap contract references a three month benchmark rate. As the benchmark rises, the floating leg payments increase, affecting the swap value.

## Practical notes
The choice of underlying rate depends on the contract specification and market convention. Market transitions between benchmarks can affect pricing and risk management.

- Using a rule on a different timeframe than it was designed for.

- Assuming the same behavior in abnormal volatility.
