# Rollover

## Introduction
Rollover is a term that is widely used in trading and [finance](../f/finance.md) to describe the process of extending the expiration or settlement date of an [open position](../o/open_position.md). This concept is applicable in various [financial markets](../f/financial_market.md), including [futures](../f/futures.md), [options](../o/options.md), and forex. The rollover process is essential for traders and investors who wish to maintain their positions beyond the current contract's expiration without incurring the costs or risks associated with closing the position and opening a new one immediately.

## Types of Rollover
Rollover can be categorized into several types, depending on the [financial instrument](../f/financial_instrument.md) involved. Below are the main types of rollover:

### Futures Rollover
In the [futures market](../f/futures_market.md), contracts have specific expiration dates. Traders who wish to maintain their positions past the contract's expiration must engage in a process known as "rollover." This involves closing the existing [futures contract](../f/futures_contract.md) that is approaching expiration and simultaneously opening a new contract with a later [expiration date](../e/expiration_date.md).

### Options Rollover
In [options](../o/options.md) trading, rollover involves moving the existing [options](../o/options.md) position to a future [expiration date](../e/expiration_date.md) by closing the current option and opening a new one with a longer [expiration date](../e/expiration_date.md). This allows traders to extend their exposure to the [asset](../a/asset.md) without letting the option expire worthless.

### Forex Rollover
In the forex [market](../m/market.md), rollover refers to the [interest](../i/interest.md) paid or earned for holding a [currency](../c/currency.md) position overnight. Known as the "[swap](../s/swap.md)" rate, this [interest](../i/interest.md) is calculated based on the [interest rate](../i/interest_rate.md) differential between the two currencies in the pair.

## Rollover Mechanics
### Futures Rollover Mechanics
The core principle in [futures](../f/futures.md) rollover is to replace the expiring contract with a contract that has a later [expiration date](../e/expiration_date.md). The mechanics involve:

1. **Identifying the Rollover Date**: Each [futures contract](../f/futures_contract.md) has a standardized [expiration date](../e/expiration_date.md). Traders need to know the recommended rollover date, which is usually a few days before the expiration.
2. **Closing the Current Position**: Traders [will](../w/will.md) close their position in the expiring contract, usually through a sell [order](../o/order.md) if they are long, or a buy [order](../o/order.md) if they are short.
3. **Opening a New Position**: Simultaneously, traders [will](../w/will.md) [open](../o/open.md) a new position in the contract with the next [expiration date](../e/expiration_date.md), maintaining a similar position size.

### Options Rollover Mechanics
The process of rolling over an option position involves several steps:

1. **Choosing the New [Expiration Date](../e/expiration_date.md)**: [Trade](../t/trade.md) the current option for one with a new [expiration date](../e/expiration_date.md).
2. **Closing the Current Option**: Execute a [market order](../m/market_order.md) to close the current option.
3. **Opening the New Option**: Simultaneously, execute a [market order](../m/market_order.md) to [open](../o/open.md) a new option with the desired [expiration date](../e/expiration_date.md).

### Forex Rollover Mechanics
In the forex [market](../m/market.md), rollover involves the following:

1. **Determining the [Swap Rate](../s/swap_rate.md)**: This rate is determined based on the [interest rate](../i/interest_rate.md) differential between the two currencies involved and is provided by the [broker](../b/broker.md).
2. **Calculating the Rollover [Fee](../f/fee.md)**: The [fee](../f/fee.md) is calculated as per the [volume](../v/volume.md) of the position and how long it is held overnight.
3. **Crediting or Debiting the Account**: The [trader](../t/trader.md)'s account [will](../w/will.md) either be credited or debited based on whether the [swap rate](../s/swap_rate.md) is positive or negative.

## Rollover Costs and Considerations
### Transaction Costs
Rollover often incurs [transaction costs](../t/transaction_costs.md), which may include [broker](../b/broker.md) fees and the [bid-ask spread](../b/bid-ask_spread.md). Traders need to [factor](../f/factor.md) in these costs when deciding to rollover a position.

### Interest Rates
In forex rollover, the [interest rate](../i/interest_rate.md) differential determines the cost or benefit of rolling over a position. Traders should keep an eye on central [bank](../b/bank.md) policies and [interest rate](../i/interest_rate.md) changes, as they impact the [swap](../s/swap.md) rates.

### Market Volatility
[Market](../m/market.md) conditions and [volatility](../v/volatility.md) can significantly impact rollover costs, especially in [futures](../f/futures.md) and [options](../o/options.md) markets. High [volatility](../v/volatility.md) can widen [bid](../b/bid.md)-ask [spreads](../s/spreads.md) and increase [transaction costs](../t/transaction_costs.md).

## Rollover Strategies
### Calendar Spread Strategy
A popular rollover strategy is the calendar spread, where traders simultaneously buy the long-dated contract and sell the near-dated contract. This strategy aims to [profit](../p/profit.md) from the difference in prices between the two contracts.

### Diagonal Spread Strategy
In [options](../o/options.md) trading, the diagonal spread is another rollover strategy. It involves taking a position in an option with a longer expiration and a different [strike price](../s/strike_price.md) than the current one. This offers more flexibility in rolling positions.

### Carry Trade Strategy
In the forex [market](../m/market.md), the [carry trade](../c/carry_trade.md) strategy exploits the [interest rate](../i/interest_rate.md) differential between two currencies. Traders borrow in a [currency](../c/currency.md) with a low [interest rate](../i/interest_rate.md) and invest in one with a higher rate, earning the rollover [interest](../i/interest.md) as [profit](../p/profit.md).

## Conclusion
Rollover is an essential concept in trading and [finance](../f/finance.md), enabling traders and investors to extend their positions beyond the current contract's expiration or settlement date.
