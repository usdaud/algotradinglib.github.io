# Payoff Diagrams

Payoff diagrams are graphical representations that traders and investors use to visualize the potential outcomes of their trades and investments at expiration. These diagrams are most commonly used in [options](../o/options.md) trading to illustrate the potential [profit](../p/profit.md) or loss of an [options](../o/options.md) position at various prices of the [underlying asset](../u/underlying_asset.md). By understanding payoff diagrams, traders can better anticipate the risks and rewards of their strategies and make more informed decisions.

## Introduction to Payoff Diagrams

Payoff diagrams plot the potential [profit](../p/profit.md) or loss (Y-axis) against the [underlying asset](../u/underlying_asset.md)'s price at expiration (X-axis). These diagrams are essential tools because they help traders visualize the outcomes of different positions and strategies, from simple call and [put options](../p/put_options.md) to more complex combinations like straddles, strangles, [spreads](../s/spreads.md), and butterflies.

The fundamental components of a payoff diagram include:
- **X-axis**: Represents the price of the [underlying asset](../u/underlying_asset.md) at expiration.
- **Y-axis**: Represents the [profit](../p/profit.md) or loss.
- **[Breakeven point](../b/breakeven_point.md)**: The point where the position neither makes nor loses [money](../m/money.md).
- **Maximum [profit](../p/profit.md) and loss**: Indicate the highest potential [gain](../g/gain.md) and maximum possible loss.

## Types of Payoff Diagrams

### 1. Long Call Option

A long [call option](../c/call_option.md) gives the holder the right, but not the obligation, to buy the [underlying asset](../u/underlying_asset.md) at a specified [strike price](../s/strike_price.md) before or at the [expiration date](../e/expiration_date.md).

- **Shape**: The payoff diagram for a long [call option](../c/call_option.md) is a hockey stick shape, starting with a flat line (indicating a loss) below the [strike price](../s/strike_price.md) and sloping upward as the [underlying](../u/underlying.md) price increases above the [strike price](../s/strike_price.md).
- **[Breakeven Point](../b/breakeven_point.md)**: The [strike price](../s/strike_price.md) plus the [premium](../p/premium.md) paid.
- **Maximum Loss**: Limited to the [premium](../p/premium.md) paid.
- **Maximum [Profit](../p/profit.md)**: Potentially unlimited, as the [underlying](../u/underlying.md) price can rise indefinitely.

!Long Call Payoff Diagram

### 2. Long Put Option

A long [put option](../p/put.md) grants the holder the right to sell the [underlying asset](../u/underlying_asset.md) at a specified [strike price](../s/strike_price.md) before or at the [expiration date](../e/expiration_date.md).

- **Shape**: The payoff diagram for a long [put option](../p/put.md) is also a hockey stick shape but inverted, with profits improving as the [underlying](../u/underlying.md) price falls below the [strike price](../s/strike_price.md).
- **[Breakeven Point](../b/breakeven_point.md)**: The [strike price](../s/strike_price.md) minus the [premium](../p/premium.md) paid.
- **Maximum Loss**: Limited to the [premium](../p/premium.md) paid.
- **Maximum [Profit](../p/profit.md)**: Limited to the [strike price](../s/strike_price.md) minus the [premium](../p/premium.md), assuming the [underlying asset](../u/underlying_asset.md) could fall to zero.

!Long Put Payoff Diagram

### 3. Covered Call

A [covered call](../c/covered_call.md) strategy involves holding a long position in the [underlying asset](../u/underlying_asset.md) while selling (writing) a [call option](../c/call_option.md) on that same [asset](../a/asset.md).

- **Shape**: The payoff diagram for a [covered call](../c/covered_call.md) involves a flat line loss up to the [strike price](../s/strike_price.md), after which profits cap due to the [short call](../s/short_call.md).
- **[Breakeven Point](../b/breakeven_point.md)**: The purchase price of the [underlying asset](../u/underlying_asset.md) minus the [premium](../p/premium.md) received.
- **Maximum Loss**: Significant, as it includes the potential for the [underlying asset](../u/underlying_asset.md) price to drop to zero minus the [premium](../p/premium.md) received.
- **Maximum [Profit](../p/profit.md)**: Limited to the [premium](../p/premium.md) received plus the difference between the purchase price of the [underlying](../u/underlying.md) and the [strike price](../s/strike_price.md).

!Covered Call Payoff Diagram

### 4. Protective Put

A [protective put](../p/protective_put.md) strategy involves holding a long position in the [underlying asset](../u/underlying_asset.md) while purchasing a [put option](../p/put.md) on that [asset](../a/asset.md) to [hedge](../h/hedge.md) against potential losses.

- **Shape**: The payoff diagram for a [protective put](../p/protective_put.md) includes unlimited downside protection and unlimited [upside potential](../u/upside_potential_in_trading.md) beyond the combined cost of the put and the [underlying](../u/underlying.md).
- **[Breakeven Point](../b/breakeven_point.md)**: The purchase price of the [underlying asset](../u/underlying_asset.md) plus the [premium](../p/premium.md) paid for the put.
- **Maximum Loss**: Limited to the purchase price of the [underlying asset](../u/underlying_asset.md) plus the cost of the put minus the [strike price](../s/strike_price.md) of the put.
- **Maximum [Profit](../p/profit.md)**: Unlimited, based on the appreciation of the [underlying asset](../u/underlying_asset.md) price minus the [premium](../p/premium.md) paid.

!Protective Put Payoff Diagram

### 5. Straddle

A [straddle](../s/straddle.md) involves simultaneously buying a call and a [put option](../p/put.md) with the same [strike price](../s/strike_price.md) and [expiration date](../e/expiration_date.md) on the same [underlying asset](../u/underlying_asset.md).

- **Shape**: The payoff diagram for a [straddle](../s/straddle.md) is V-shaped, with potential for unlimited [gain](../g/gain.md) if the [underlying asset](../u/underlying_asset.md)'s price moves significantly in either direction from the [strike price](../s/strike_price.md).
- **Breakeven Points**: The [strike price](../s/strike_price.md) plus and minus the combined [premium](../p/premium.md) paid for both [options](../o/options.md).
- **Maximum Loss**: Limited to the combined premiums paid for the call and put.
- **Maximum [Profit](../p/profit.md)**: Unlimited, as long as the [underlying](../u/underlying.md) moves substantially either up or down.

!Straddle Payoff Diagram

## Advanced Strategies and Combinations

Payoff diagrams become more complex when combining [multiple](../m/multiple.md) [options](../o/options.md) positions, often aimed at fine-tuning [risk](../r/risk.md) and reward profiles. Here are a few common sophisticated strategies:

### 1. Bull Call Spread

A [bull call spread](../b/bull_call_spread.md) involves buying a [call option](../c/call_option.md) at a lower [strike price](../s/strike_price.md) while selling another [call option](../c/call_option.md) at a higher [strike price](../s/strike_price.md) on the same [underlying asset](../u/underlying_asset.md) and [expiration date](../e/expiration_date.md).

- **Shape**: The payoff diagram for a [bull call spread](../b/bull_call_spread.md) starts with a maximum loss limited to the [net premium](../n/net_premium.md) paid if the [underlying](../u/underlying.md) price is below the lower strike. The [profit](../p/profit.md) is capped between the lower and upper strikes.
- **[Breakeven Point](../b/breakeven_point.md)**: The lower [strike price](../s/strike_price.md) plus the [net premium](../n/net_premium.md) paid.
- **Maximum Loss**: Limited to the [net premium](../n/net_premium.md) paid.
- **Maximum [Profit](../p/profit.md)**: Limited to the difference between the strike prices minus the [net premium](../n/net_premium.md) paid.

!Bull Call Spread Payoff Diagram

### 2. Bear Put Spread

A [bear put spread](../b/bear_put_spread.md) involves buying a [put option](../p/put.md) at a higher [strike price](../s/strike_price.md) while selling another [put option](../p/put.md) at a lower [strike price](../s/strike_price.md) on the same [underlying asset](../u/underlying_asset.md) and [expiration date](../e/expiration_date.md).

- **Shape**: The payoff diagram for a [bear put spread](../b/bear_put_spread.md) starts with the maximum potential loss limited to the [net premium](../n/net_premium.md) if the [underlying](../u/underlying.md) price is above the higher strike. The [profit](../p/profit.md) is capped between the lower and higher strikes.
- **[Breakeven Point](../b/breakeven_point.md)**: The higher [strike price](../s/strike_price.md) minus the [net premium](../n/net_premium.md) paid.
- **Maximum Loss**: Limited to the [net premium](../n/net_premium.md) paid.
- **Maximum [Profit](../p/profit.md)**: Limited to the difference between the strike prices minus the [net premium](../n/net_premium.md) paid.

!Bear Put Spread Payoff Diagram

### 3. Iron Condor

An [iron condor](../i/iron_condor.md) involves selling a call spread and a put spread with the same expiration dates but different strike prices. Typically, the sold calls and puts are closer to the [underlying](../u/underlying.md) price, while the purchased [options](../o/options.md) are further out.

- **Shape**: The payoff diagram for an [iron condor](../i/iron_condor.md) has a flat maximum [profit](../p/profit.md) region, a loss region on both sides, and capped risks at extreme price movements of the [underlying asset](../u/underlying_asset.md).
- **Breakeven Points**: Two pointsâ€”one between the lower sold and bought put strikes and one between the sold and bought call strikes.
- **Maximum Loss**: Limited to the differences in strike prices minus the [net premium](../n/net_premium.md) received.
- **Maximum [Profit](../p/profit.md)**: Limited to the [net premium](../n/net_premium.md) received.

!Iron Condor Payoff Diagram

### 4. Butterfly Spread

A [butterfly spread](../b/butterfly_spread.md) involves buying a call (or put) at a lower strike, selling two calls (or puts) at a middle strike, and buying another call (or put) at a higher strike. The middle strike is usually centered between the other two.

- **Shape**: The payoff diagram for a [butterfly spread](../b/butterfly_spread.md) has a peak indicating the highest [profit](../p/profit.md) at the middle [strike price](../s/strike_price.md) and decreasing [profit](../p/profit.md) towards the wings.
- **Breakeven Points**: Two points between the lower and middle strike and the middle and upper strike.
- **Maximum Loss**: Limited to the [net premium](../n/net_premium.md) paid.
- **Maximum [Profit](../p/profit.md)**: Limited to the difference between the middle strike and either outer strike minus the [net premium](../n/net_premium.md) paid.

!Butterfly Spread Payoff Diagram

## Applications and Considerations

When using payoff diagrams, it's critical to keep in mind certain applications and considerations:

- **[Risk Management](../r/risk_management.md)**: Payoff diagrams help traders assess and manage risks, setting realistic expectations for profitable and adverse outcomes.
- **Strategy Assessment**: By visualizing different strategies and combinations, traders can determine which approach aligns best with their [market](../m/market.md) outlook and [risk tolerance](../r/risk_tolerance.md).
- **Hedging**: Advanced strategies like protective puts or iron condors are often used for hedging existing positions.
- **[Volatility](../v/volatility.md)**: Understanding how [volatility](../v/volatility.md) affects [options](../o/options.md) prices (and thus payoffs) is essential for using payoff diagrams effectively.
- **[Time Decay](../t/time_decay.md)**: [Time decay](../t/time_decay.md) ([theta](../t/theta.md)) affects [options](../o/options.md), and its impact can be visualized through payoff diagrams as expiration approaches.

In conclusion, payoff diagrams are versatile tools in the arsenal of investors and traders, providing critical insights into the [profit](../p/profit.md) and loss potential of various strategies. By analyzing these diagrams, individuals can align their trading decisions more closely with their financial goals and [risk profiles](../r/risk_profiles.md).

For further information on how payoff diagrams can be used for specific [trading strategies](../t/trading_strategies.md), you can explore comprehensive educational resources from [options](../o/options.md) trading institutions such as Cboe Global Markets, leaders in facilitating [options](../o/options.md) trading and education.
