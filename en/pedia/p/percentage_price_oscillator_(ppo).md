# Percentage Price Oscillator (PPO)

The percentage price oscillator is a momentum indicator similar to the MACD, but expressed as a percentage. It measures the difference between two exponential moving averages as a percent of the longer average.

## Formula
PPO = ((EMA fast - EMA slow) / EMA slow) * 100

A signal line, often a 9 period EMA of the PPO, is used to generate crossover signals.

## Interpretation
- Positive values indicate the fast EMA is above the slow EMA.
- Negative values indicate the fast EMA is below the slow EMA.
- Crossovers can signal momentum shifts.

## Example
The PPO crosses above zero after a long decline, suggesting a shift toward bullish momentum. A trader watches for confirmation with price action.

## Practical notes
PPO allows comparison across assets with different price levels because it is percentage based.

- Treating Percentage Price Oscillator (PPO) as a standalone signal instead of context.
- Ignoring liquidity, spreads, and execution friction.
- Using a rule on a different timeframe than it was designed for.
- Overfitting a small sample of past examples.
- Assuming the same behavior in abnormal volatility.
