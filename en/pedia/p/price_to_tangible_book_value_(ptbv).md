# Price to Tangible Book Value (PTBV)

Price to Tangible [Book Value](../b/book_value.md) (PTBV) is a financial metric used to compare a company's [market price](../m/market_price.md) to its tangible [book value](../b/book_value.md). It is particularly valuable to investors as it provides a more clear and tangible measurement of a company's worth than the traditional Price to [Book Value](../b/book_value.md) (P/B) ratio. This metric is especially significant in financial industries like banking, [insurance](../i/insurance.md), and investment companies where [tangible assets](../t/tangible_asset.md) [hold](../h/hold.md) greater importance.

## What is Tangible Book Value?

Tangible [Book Value](../b/book_value.md) (TBV) refers to the [value](../v/value.md) of a company's physical assets excluding any intangible assets and liabilities. Intangible assets include items like patents, trademarks, [goodwill](../g/goodwill.md), and other non-physical assets. The formula to calculate TBV is:

\[ \text{TBV} = \text{Total Assets} - \text{[Total Liabilities](../t/total_liabilities.md)} - \text{Intangible Assets} \]

## Calculating PTBV

The PTBV ratio is calculated by dividing the company's current share price by its tangible [book value](../b/book_value.md) per share (TBVPS). The formula is:

\[ \text{PTBV} = \frac{\text{[Market Price](../m/market_price.md) per Share}}{\text{Tangible [Book Value](../b/book_value.md) per Share}} \]

Where Tangible [Book Value](../b/book_value.md) per Share (TBVPS) is:

\[ \text{TBVPS} = \frac{\text{Tangible [Book Value](../b/book_value.md)}}{\text{Total Number of Outstanding [Shares](../s/shares.md)}} \]

## Importance of PTBV

1. **[Valuation](../v/valuation.md) [Indicator](../i/indicator.md):** PTBV helps in assessing whether a company's stock is [overvalued](../o/overvalued.md) or [undervalued](../u/undervalued.md). A lower PTBV ratio suggests that the stock is [undervalued](../u/undervalued.md) compared to its [tangible assets](../t/tangible_asset.md), making it potentially attractive for investors. Conversely, a higher PTBV ratio could indicate overvaluation.

2. **Focus on [Tangible Assets](../t/tangible_asset.md):** By excluding intangible assets, PTBV provides a clearer picture of a company's [value](../v/value.md) backed by actual physical assets. This is crucial for industries like banking where [tangible assets](../t/tangible_asset.md) are paramount.

3. **[Risk](../r/risk.md) Assessment:** A low PTBV ratio might indicate that a company is trading at a [discount](../d/discount.md) to its tangible [book value](../b/book_value.md), suggesting lower [risk](../r/risk.md). However, it can also signal potential issues with the company's future profitability or [market](../m/market.md) perception.

## PTBV vs. P/B Ratio

While both PTBV and P/B are [valuation](../v/valuation.md) metrics, PTBV is more stringent because it excludes intangible assets. The P/B ratio, which includes intangible assets, can often paint an overly optimistic picture of a company's worth. PTBV is particularly useful for conservative investors who prefer a safety [margin](../m/margin.md) based on concrete, [tangible assets](../t/tangible_asset.md).

## Limitations of PTBV

1. **Not Suitable for All Industries:** PTBV is less effective for industries heavily reliant on intangible assets, such as technology or healthcare, where intellectual properties and patents form critical [business](../b/business.md) components.

2. **[Market Sentiment](../m/market_sentiment.md):** [Market price](../m/market_price.md) can be influenced by [investor](../i/investor.md) sentiment, [speculation](../s/speculation.md), and external factors, which can distort the true [valuation](../v/valuation.md) indicated by PTBV.

3. **[Asset Valuation](../a/asset_valuation.md) Issues:** Determining the tangible [book value](../b/book_value.md) can sometimes be complicated due to [asset](../a/asset.md) [depreciation](../d/depreciation.md), obsolescence, and varying [accounting](../a/accounting.md) practices.

## Conclusion

Price to Tangible [Book Value](../b/book_value.md) (PTBV) provides a [robust](../r/robust.md) framework for investors looking to understand a company's [market](../m/market.md) [valuation](../v/valuation.md) relative to its physical assets. Especially useful in the banking, [real estate](../r/real_estate.md), and [insurance](../i/insurance.md) sectors, PTBV helps in [risk](../r/risk.md) assessment and identifying [undervalued](../u/undervalued.md) investment opportunities. However, its limitations in certain industries and its dependency on accurate [asset valuation](../a/asset_valuation.md) must be taken into consideration.
