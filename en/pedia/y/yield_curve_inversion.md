# Yield Curve Inversion

A yield curve inversion occurs when short term interest rates are higher than long term rates. This is the opposite of the normal upward sloping curve.

## Causes
Inversions often occur when central banks raise short term rates while investors expect slower growth or lower inflation. Demand for long term bonds can push long term yields down, creating the inversion.

## Market Implications
Yield curve inversions are often viewed as recession warnings, though timing is uncertain. They can affect credit spreads, equity valuations, and sector performance. Fixed income strategies may shift toward duration or curve trades.

## Trading Considerations
Traders monitor the shape of the curve and the spread between key maturities. Curve inversions can influence positioning in interest rate futures, swaps, and bond portfolios. Risk management is important because curve signals can persist for long periods.

## Conclusion
Yield curve inversion is a significant macro signal, but it is not a precise timing tool. It should be interpreted alongside broader economic indicators.
