# Vienna Stock Exchange (WBAG).VI

The Vienna Stock [Exchange](../e/exchange.md) (Wiener BÃ¶rse or WBAG) is the primary stock [exchange](../e/exchange.md) in Austria. Established in 1771 by Empress Maria Theresa of Austria, it is one of the oldest stock exchanges in the world. It plays a significant role in the financial [market](../m/market.md) of Austria and Central Europe by [offering](../o/offering.md) a variety of financial instruments including [equity](../e/equity.md), bonds, and [derivatives](../d/derivatives.md).

## Historical Background

The Vienna Stock [Exchange](../e/exchange.md) was established over two centuries ago, showcasing the long-standing tradition of [financial markets](../f/financial_market.md) in Austria. Initially, it was primarily focused on government bonds, but over time, it expanded to include equities, mutual funds, and various [derivative](../d/derivative.md) products.

## Structure and Organization

The Vienna Stock [Exchange](../e/exchange.md) operates various [market](../m/market.md) segments:
- **[Equity Market](../e/equity_market.md)**: Includes the main listings of Austrian companies.
- **[Bond Market](../b/bond_market.md)**: Features government and corporate bonds.
- **[Derivative](../d/derivative.md) [Market](../m/market.md)**: Offers [futures](../f/futures.md) and [options](../o/options.md) on indices and [stocks](../s/stock.md).
- **New [Market](../m/market.md)**: A segment for young, high-growth companies.

Each segment is designed to cater to different types of investors and varying [risk](../r/risk.md) appetites.

## Market Indices

The Vienna Stock [Exchange](../e/exchange.md) is known for several key indices that track the performance of [listed](../l/listed.md) securities:
- **ATX (Austrian Traded [Index](../i/index_instrument.md))**: The premier [index](../i/index_instrument.md) that comprises the 20 largest and most traded [stocks](../s/stock.md).
- **ATX Prime**: Encompasses a broader [range](../r/range.md) of companies and is used as a [benchmark](../b/benchmark.md).
- **CECE [Index](../i/index_instrument.md)**: Tracks companies in Central, Eastern, and Southeastern Europe.
