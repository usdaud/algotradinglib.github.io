# Wallpaper Stock / Wallpaper Bonds

## Definition
"Wallpaper stock" or "wallpaper bonds" are colloquial terms used to describe [stocks](../s/stock.md) or bonds that have become worthless or nearly worthless, often due to company [bankruptcy](../b/bankruptcy.md) or severe [financial distress](../f/financial_distress.md).

## Key Characteristics

### 1. Minimal Value
- The securities have little to no monetary [value](../v/value.md)
- Often traded for pennies, if traded at all

### 2. Historical Context
- Typically refers to physical stock or [bond](../b/bond.md) certificates
- Named because the certificates are only useful as decorative wallpaper

### 3. Failed Investments
- Result of significant company or economic failures
- Often associated with [market](../m/market.md) crashes or [industry](../i/industry.md) collapses

## Historical Significance

1. **[Great Depression](../g/great_depression.md) Era**
 - Many [stocks](../s/stock.md) became "wallpaper" after the 1929 [stock market crash](../s/stock_market_crash.md)
 - Symbolized lost fortunes and economic devastation

2. **Dot-com Bubble**
 - Tech [stocks](../s/stock.md) that became worthless after the 2000 bubble burst
 - Modern example of "wallpaper [stocks](../s/stock.md)"

3. **2008 [Financial Crisis](../f/financial_crisis.md)**
 - Some financial institution [stocks](../s/stock.md) and [mortgage](../m/mortgage.md)-backed securities became nearly worthless

## Financial Implications

### 1. Total Loss for Investors
- Represents a complete or near-complete loss of investment

### 2. Tax Considerations
- May be used for [tax loss harvesting](../t/tax_loss_harvesting.md)
- Can be written off as [capital](../c/capital.md) losses in some jurisdictions

### 3. Potential for Fraud
- [Worthless securities](../w/worthless_securities.md) sometimes used in "pump and dump" schemes
- Regulators warn investors about risks of trading in extremely low-[value](../v/value.md) [stocks](../s/stock.md)

## Psychological Impact

1. **[Investor](../i/investor.md) Sentiment**
 - Can lead to long-term [investor](../i/investor.md) distrust in certain sectors or markets
 - Often used as cautionary tales in investment education

2. **[Market](../m/market.md) Psychology**
 - Contributes to fear during [market](../m/market.md) downturns
 - Can influence [risk](../r/risk.md) perception in future investments

## Modern Context

1. **Digital Era**
 - Less common with the shift to electronic trading and record-keeping
 - Term still used metaphorically for worthless digital assets

2. **Cryptocurrency**
 - Some failed cryptocurrencies likened to "digital wallpaper"
 - Highlights [volatility](../v/volatility.md) and [risk](../r/risk.md) in new financial technologies
