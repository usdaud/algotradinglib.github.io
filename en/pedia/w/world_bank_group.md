# World Bank Group

The [World Bank](../w/world_bank.md) Group (WBG) is a unique global partnership comprising five institutions that collectively aim to reduce [poverty](../p/poverty.md) and support development. Established in 1944 during the Bretton Woods Conference, the [World Bank](../w/world_bank.md) Group provides a [range](../r/range.md) of financial products and policy advice to developing countries.

## Institutions of the World Bank Group

### 1. International Bank for Reconstruction and Development (IBRD)
The IBRD lends to governments of middle-[income](../i/income.md) and creditworthy low-[income](../i/income.md) countries. It provides [finance](../f/finance.md) for public administration, [infrastructure](../i/infrastructure.md), and other projects that are expected to improve economic prospects and [quality of life](../q/quality_of_life.md). The IBRD raises most of its funds on the world’s [financial markets](../f/financial_market.md).

### 2. International Development Association (IDA)
The IDA focuses on the world’s poorest countries, [offering](../o/offering.md) concessional loans and grants. Since 1960, IDA has provided approximately $369 billion for investments in 113 countries. IDA funds enhance education, healthcare, [infrastructure](../i/infrastructure.md), agriculture, and economic policy improvements.

### 3. International Finance Corporation (IFC)
The IFC is the largest global development institution focused on the [private sector](../p/private_sector.md) in developing countries. It provides investment, advice, and [asset management](../a/asset_management.md) services to encourage private enterprise development. By promoting [private sector](../p/private_sector.md) investments, the IFC aims to create markets and opportunities where they are needed most.

### 4. Multilateral Investment Guarantee Agency (MIGA)
MIGA provides [political risk](../p/political_risk.md) [insurance](../i/insurance.md) and [credit](../c/credit.md) enhancement to encourage [foreign investment](../f/foreign_investment.md) in developing countries. It aims to promote [economic growth](../e/economic_growth.md) by mitigating the risks associated with [foreign investment](../f/foreign_investment.md), such as [expropriation](../e/expropriation.md), [currency](../c/currency.md) inconvertibility, and civil unrest.

### 5. International Centre for Settlement of Investment Disputes (ICSID)
ICSID provides facilities for the [arbitration](../a/arbitration.md) and conciliation of international investment disputes. It is dedicated to fostering an environment of mutual respect and legal certainty for foreign investors and host countries, enhancing the confidence of global investors.

## Structure and Governance

### Board of Governors
The WBG is governed by the [Board of Governors](../b/board_of_governors.md), where each member country appoints a governor, usually its [finance](../f/finance.md) minister or central [bank](../b/bank.md) governor. The [Board of Governors](../b/board_of_governors.md) meets annually.

### Board of Executive Directors
The Board of Executive Directors is responsible for the general operations of the WBG. It comprises 25 Executive Directors, representing a constituency of countries. They meet regularly and are instrumental in decision-making processes regarding loans, policies, and strategies.

### President of the World Bank Group
The president oversees the [World Bank](../w/world_bank.md) Group's work, coordinates with member countries, and serves as a key representative at global forums. The president is elected for a renewable five-year term by the Board of Executive Directors.

## Strategic Goals

### Ending Extreme Poverty
The WBG aims to reduce the percentage of people living on less than $1.90 a day to 3% by 2030. It focuses on fostering inclusive growth through investments in [human capital](../h/human_capital.md), sustainable [infrastructure](../i/infrastructure.md), and private-sector development.

### Boosting Shared Prosperity
The WBG strives to increase the [income](../i/income.md) growth of the bottom 40% of each country's population. It supports universal access to essential services, quality education, and [robust](../r/robust.md) social protections.

## Financial Products and Services

### Loans and Credits
The [World Bank](../w/world_bank.md) provides two types of financial products:
- **Investment Project [Financing](../f/financing.md) (IPF):** This supports [infrastructure](../i/infrastructure.md) and service delivery projects over an extended period.
- **Development Policy [Financing](../f/financing.md) (DPF):** This provides quick-disbursing [budget](../b/budget.md) support based on policy and institutional actions taken by the borrower.

### Guarantees
The WBG offers guarantees to facilitate private investment in countries experiencing financial constraints. These guarantees cover risks that governments or businesses may face, promoting confidence among international investors.

### Grants
Grants are offered to foster projects and initiatives that directly tackle global challenges or crises, such as health emergencies or disasters.

## Areas of Focus

### Education
The WBG directs funds and expertise to improving education systems, ensuring inclusive and equitable quality education for all.

### Health
Healthcare is a priority, with efforts to enhance health systems, respond to pandemics, and ensure universal health coverage.

### Infrastructure
Investment in transportation, energy, water, and digital [infrastructure](../i/infrastructure.md) drives economic development. The WBG aims to build resilient and sustainable [infrastructure](../i/infrastructure.md).

### Climate Change
The WBG emphasizes climate-related initiatives, helping countries adapt to and mitigate the impacts of climate change through sustainable development projects.

## Collaboration with Other Organizations

The WBG collaborates with numerous international organizations, including the United Nations, International Monetary [Fund](../f/fund.md) (IMF), regional development banks, and non-governmental organizations (NGOs). These collaborations aim to amplify developmental impacts and mobilize additional resources.

## Innovation and Technology

### Fintech
The [World Bank](../w/world_bank.md) Group supports Fintech initiatives that expand [financial inclusion](../f/financial_inclusion.md), make banking services more accessible, and spur [economic growth](../e/economic_growth.md). It remains at the forefront of promoting digital payments, [blockchain](../b/blockchain_in_trading.md), and other innovative financial technologies.

### Data and Analytics
By [investing](../i/investing.md) in data collection, analytics, and dissemination, the WBG enables more efficient and effective decision-making. This evidence-based approach fosters better project outcomes and policy formulations.

## Addressing Global Challenges

### Pandemics and Health Crises
In response to health crises such as the COVID-19 pandemic, the WBG allocates financial resources and expertise to support healthcare systems, provide emergency funding, and foster global cooperation.

### Natural Disasters and Resilience
The [World Bank](../w/world_bank.md) Group aids in disaster recovery and builds resilience by [financing](../f/financing.md) reconstruction and implementing early warning systems.

### Migration and Refugee Challenges
Support for displaced persons and communities affected by migration involves socioeconomic integration and [provision](../p/provision.md) of essential services.

## Criticisms and Challenges

### Environmental and Social Impacts
Critics argue that some [World Bank](../w/world_bank.md)-financed projects have had adverse environmental and social effects. The WBG has revisited its safeguards to ensure environmentally and socially sustainable development.

### Debt Sustainability
Concerns have been raised about the [debt](../d/debt.md) sustainability of borrowing countries, given the significant loans extended by the IBRD and IDA.

### Governance and Transparency
The governance structure of the WBG, influenced by the voting power of member countries, has been a point of contention, with calls for increased [transparency](../t/transparency.md) and representation of developing nations.

## Conclusion
The [World Bank](../w/world_bank.md) Group remains a pivotal player in global development, dedicated to tackling [poverty](../p/poverty.md) and fostering sustainable [economic growth](../e/economic_growth.md). Its multifaceted approach, involving financial support, policy advice, and global collaboration, positions it as a cornerstone institution in the international development landscape.
