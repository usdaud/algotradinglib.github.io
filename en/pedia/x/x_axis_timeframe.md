# X Axis Timeframe

X axis timeframe refers to the time scale shown on the horizontal axis of a chart. It defines how much time each bar or candle represents and sets the context for analysis.

## Examples
- A 1 minute chart shows each bar as one minute of trading.
- A daily chart shows each bar as one trading day.
- A weekly chart compresses data into week long bars.

## Why it matters
The timeframe influences signals and interpretation. Patterns on a short timeframe may be noise on a higher timeframe, while long term trends can be invisible on a very short chart.

## Example
A stock may look range bound on a 5 minute chart but trending on a daily chart. The chosen timeframe affects trade decisions.

## Practical notes
Traders often analyze multiple timeframes to align short term entries with longer term trends.

## Practical checklist
- Define the time horizon for X Axis Timeframe and the market context.
- Identify the data inputs you trust, such as price, volume, or schedule dates.
- Write a clear entry and exit rule before committing capital.
- Size the position so a single error does not damage the account.
- Document the result to improve repeatability.

## Common pitfalls
- Treating X Axis Timeframe as a standalone signal instead of context.
- Ignoring liquidity, spreads, and execution friction.
- Using a rule on a different timeframe than it was designed for.
- Overfitting a small sample of past examples.
- Assuming the same behavior in abnormal volatility.
