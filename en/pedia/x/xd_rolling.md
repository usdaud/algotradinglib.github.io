# XD Rolling

XD rolling refers to the process of rolling a position around the ex dividend date. The term is used in some derivatives and equity strategies that seek to manage dividend exposure.

## Concept
When a stock goes ex dividend, its price typically drops by the dividend amount. Traders may roll positions to avoid or capture the dividend effect depending on their strategy.

## Example
An options trader holds a call spread and decides to roll the position to a later expiration before the ex dividend date to reduce assignment risk.

## Practical notes
Dividend related effects can influence option pricing and early exercise decisions. Strategies should consider dividend schedules and borrow costs.

## Practical checklist
- Define the time horizon for XD Rolling and the market context.
- Identify the data inputs you trust, such as price, volume, or schedule dates.
- Write a clear entry and exit rule before committing capital.
- Size the position so a single error does not damage the account.
- Document the result to improve repeatability.

## Common pitfalls
- Treating XD Rolling as a standalone signal instead of context.
- Ignoring liquidity, spreads, and execution friction.
- Using a rule on a different timeframe than it was designed for.
- Overfitting a small sample of past examples.
- Assuming the same behavior in abnormal volatility.

## Data and measurement
Good analysis starts with consistent data. For XD Rolling, confirm the data source, the time zone, and the sampling frequency. If the concept depends on settlement or schedule dates, align the calendar with the exchange rules. If it depends on price action, consider using adjusted data to handle corporate actions.

## Risk management notes
Risk control is essential when applying XD Rolling. Define the maximum loss per trade, the total exposure across related positions, and the conditions that invalidate the idea. A plan for fast exits is useful when markets move sharply.
