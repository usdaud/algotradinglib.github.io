# Zerodha

## Overview
Zerodha is India's largest retail [stockbroker](../s/stockbroker.md), [offering](../o/offering.md) a wide [range](../r/range.md) of financial services including [equity trading](../e/equity_trading.md), [derivatives](../d/derivatives.md) trading, commodities, mutual funds, and bonds. Known for its [discount](../d/discount.md) brokerage model, advanced trading platforms, and comprehensive educational resources, Zerodha caters to individual investors, traders, and institutions.

## Features
1. **Wide [Range](../r/range.md) of Instruments**: Access to equities, [derivatives](../d/derivatives.md), commodities, mutual funds, and bonds.
2. **[Discount](../d/discount.md) Brokerage**: Low-cost trading with a flat [fee](../f/fee.md) per [trade](../t/trade.md), making it one of the most cost-effective brokers in India.
3. **Advanced Trading Platforms**: Offers [multiple](../m/multiple.md) trading platforms including Kite, Coin, Console, and Kite Mobile with advanced charting tools and real-time data.
4. **Educational Resources**: Extensive educational materials, including webinars, tutorials, articles, and [market](../m/market.md) analysis through Zerodha Varsity.
5. **[Market Research](../m/market_research.md) and Analysis**: Access to comprehensive research reports, [market](../m/market.md) news, and advanced analysis tools.
6. **API Access**: [Robust](../r/robust.md) APIs for automated trading, custom integrations, and developing third-party trading applications.
7. **[Portfolio Management](../p/portfolio_management.md)**: Tools for tracking and managing investment portfolios efficiently.
8. **Mobile Trading**: Mobile apps for iOS and Android devices, allowing trading on the go.
9. **[Mutual Fund](../m/mutual_fund.md) Investments**: Platform for [investing](../i/investing.md) in direct mutual funds without any [commission](../c/commission.md).
10. **Secure and Regulated**: Zerodha is regulated by top-tier financial authorities in India, ensuring a secure [trading environment](../t/trading_environment.md).

## Key Components
- **Kite**: A powerful [trading platform](../t/trading_platform.md) [offering](../o/offering.md) advanced charting, real-time data, and a [range](../r/range.md) of trading tools.
- **Kite Mobile**: Mobile application for trading, account management, and [market research](../m/market_research.md) on the go.
- **Coin**: A platform for [investing](../i/investing.md) in direct mutual funds.
- **Console**: A back-office platform for tracking investments and generating reports.
- **Educational Resources (Varsity)**: A library of educational content, including webinars, tutorials, and articles.
- **API Access**: APIs for automated trading and custom integrations.

## Use Cases
- **Individual Investors**: Utilized by individual investors for managing their investments, accessing research, and trading in various markets.
- **Active Traders**: Provides active traders with powerful trading tools, [real-time market data](../r/real-time_market_data.md), and advanced analysis.
- **New Traders**: Supports new traders with educational resources and a user-friendly platform.
- **Cost-Conscious Investors**: Appeals to investors looking to minimize [trading costs](../t/trading_costs.md) with a [discount](../d/discount.md) brokerage model.

## Regulation
Zerodha is regulated by several top-tier financial authorities in India, ensuring a secure and compliant [trading environment](../t/trading_environment.md):
- Securities and [Exchange](../e/exchange.md) Board of India (SEBI)
- National Stock [Exchange](../e/exchange.md) (NSE)
- Bombay Stock [Exchange](../e/exchange.md) (BSE)
- Multi [Commodity](../c/commodity.md) [Exchange](../e/exchange.md) (MCX)

## Practical checklist
- Define the time horizon for Zerodha and the market context.
- Identify the data inputs you trust, such as price, volume, or schedule dates.
- Write a clear entry and exit rule before committing capital.
- Size the position so a single error does not damage the account.
- Document the result to improve repeatability.

## Common pitfalls
- Treating Zerodha as a standalone signal instead of context.
- Ignoring liquidity, spreads, and execution friction.
- Using a rule on a different timeframe than it was designed for.
- Overfitting a small sample of past examples.
- Assuming the same behavior in abnormal volatility.
