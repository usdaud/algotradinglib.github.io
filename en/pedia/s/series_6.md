# Series 6

The Series 6 exam, also known as the [Investment Company](../i/investment_company.md) Products/Variable Contracts Limited Representative Qualification Examination, is an important credential for professionals in the financial services [industry](../i/industry.md). This exam is administered by the Financial [Industry](../i/industry.md) Regulatory Authority (FINRA), and it qualifies individuals to sell specific types of investment products, including mutual funds, variable annuities, and other [insurance](../i/insurance.md)-linked investments. The exam is essential for those who aim to build a career in financial advising, investment planning, or related fields.

## Overview

The Series 6 exam is designed to test a candidate's knowledge and understanding of the investment products they are permitted to sell, as well as the regulatory environment and ethical considerations. Given its specialized nature, the Series 6 exam is narrower in [scope](../s/scope.md) compared to other financial regulatory exams, such as the [Series 7](../s/series_7.md) exam, which covers a broader [range](../r/range.md) of investment products and services.

### Coverage of the Series 6 Exam

The Series 6 exam covers four primary areas:

1. **Regulatory Fundamentals**: Understanding the laws, regulations, and rules that govern the securities [industry](../i/industry.md) and the roles and responsibilities of representatives.
2. **[Investment Company](../i/investment_company.md) Products**: Knowledge of mutual funds, including their structures, characteristics, benefits, and risks.
3. **Variable Contracts**: Overview and details of variable annuities and other variable [insurance](../i/insurance.md) products.
4. **[Customer](../c/customer.md) Relations and Ethics**: Skills in building and maintaining client relationships, ensuring ethical practices, and proper handling of [customer](../c/customer.md) accounts.

### Purpose of the Series 6 Exam

The Series 6 exam aims to ensure that professionals in the financial services [industry](../i/industry.md) have the requisite knowledge to advise clients appropriately and manage investments effectively. Passing the Series 6 exam allows an individual to work as a registered representative for securities firms, [insurance](../i/insurance.md) companies, and other financial institutions.

## Examination Structure

### Format and Length

- **Number of Questions**: The Series 6 exam consists of 50 [multiple](../m/multiple.md)-choice questions.
- **Time Allotted**: Candidates have 90 minutes to complete the exam.
- **Passing Score**: A score of at least 70% is required to pass the Series 6 exam.

### Question Breakdown

The exam questions are divided into four distinct sections, each addressing different competencies and knowledge areas.

1. **Seeks [Business](../b/business.md) for the [Broker-Dealer](../b/broker-dealer.md) from Customers and Potential Customers** (24% of the exam)
2. **Evaluates Customers' Financial Profile and Investment Objectives** (14% of the exam)
3. **Provides Information, Investment Advice, and Makes Suitable Recommendations** (50% of the exam)
4. **Maintains Appropriate Records and Transfers of Accounts** (12% of the exam)

### Study Resources and Preparation

Candidates typically prepare for the Series 6 exam using a combination of study materials, including textbooks, online courses, practice exams, and study guides.

## Key Concepts

### Regulatory Fundamentals

This section focuses on the regulatory environment of the securities [industry](../i/industry.md). Candidates must familiarize themselves with the [Securities Exchange Act of 1934](../s/securities_exchange_act_of_1934.md), the [Investment Company Act of 1940](../i/investment_company_act_of_1940.md), and FINRA rules, among others. This includes understanding the registration requirements, [disclosure](../d/disclosure.md) [obligations](../o/obligation.md), and the anti-[fraud](../f/fraud.md) provisions applicable to the [sale](../s/sale.md) of securities.

### Investment Company Products

[Investment company](../i/investment_company.md) products include mutual funds, closed-end funds, and [exchange](../e/exchange.md)-traded funds (ETFs). Candidates must understand the structure of these investment vehicles, including their share classes, pricing, fees, expenses, and the role of a [fund manager](../f/fund_manager.md).

### Variable Contracts

Variable contracts primarily refer to variable annuities and [variable life insurance](../v/variable_life_insurance.md) products. Candidates must learn about the features, benefits, and risks associated with these products. This includes understanding the investment [options](../o/options.md), surrender charges, mortality and [expense](../e/expense.md) [risk](../r/risk.md) charges, and the tax implications for the policyholders.

### Customer Relations and Ethics

Building and maintaining strong client relationships is crucial for financial representatives. This section covers the ethical considerations and regulatory requirements for handling [customer](../c/customer.md) accounts, maintaining confidentiality, and ensuring that investment recommendations are suitable based on the [customer](../c/customer.md)’s financial profile and investment objectives.

## Ethical Considerations

Ethics play a significant role in the financial services [industry](../i/industry.md). FINRA's Code of Conduct requires professionals to act in the best interests of their clients, avoiding conflicts of [interest](../i/interest.md) and ensuring [transparency](../t/transparency.md) in all dealings. This includes the [disclosure](../d/disclosure.md) of potential risks, fees, and any conflicts of [interest](../i/interest.md) that might affect the client’s investments.

## Career Paths and Opportunities

Obtaining the Series 6 license opens up various career opportunities in the financial services sector. Professionals can work as:

- **Financial Advisors**: [Offering](../o/offering.md) investment advice and planning services to clients.
- **Registered Representatives**: Working for [broker](../b/broker.md)-dealers, selling mutual funds and variable contracts.
- **[Insurance](../i/insurance.md) Agents**: Focusing on [variable life insurance](../v/variable_life_insurance.md) and annuity products.
- **Retirement Plan Specialists**: Advising on retirement plans and investment [options](../o/options.md).

## Continuing Education

After passing the Series 6 exam and becoming a registered representative, continuous education is necessary to maintain the license. FINRA requires registered representatives to complete continuing education programs periodically to stay updated on regulatory changes, financial products, and ethical practices.

## Conclusion

The Series 6 license is a critical credential for professionals aiming to specialize in the [sale](../s/sale.md) of mutual funds, variable annuities, and [insurance](../i/insurance.md)-linked investment products. With a focus on regulatory knowledge, [investment product](../i/investment_product.md) competency, ethical practices, and client relations, the Series 6 exam ensures that representatives are well-equipped to guide their clients in making informed investment decisions. Aspiring financial professionals must invest sufficient time and effort in their preparation to successfully navigate the exam and advance their careers in the financial services [industry](../i/industry.md).
