# Short Straddle

A short [straddle](../s/straddle.md) is an [options](../o/options.md) [trading strategy](../t/trading_strategy.md) that involves writing (selling) both a [call option](../c/call_option.md) and a [put option](../p/put.md) on the same [underlying asset](../u/underlying_asset.md) with the same [strike price](../s/strike_price.md) and [expiration date](../e/expiration_date.md). This strategy is most effective in a [neutral](../n/neutral.md) [market](../m/market.md), where the [trader](../t/trader.md) believes that the price of the [underlying asset](../u/underlying_asset.md) [will](../w/will.md) not experience significant movement in either direction by the time the [options](../o/options.md) expire. Here, we [will](../w/will.md) delve into various aspects of the short [straddle](../s/straddle.md) strategy, including its components, how it works, its profitability, and associated risks.

## Components of a Short Straddle

1. **[Underlying Asset](../u/underlying_asset.md)**: The financial [security](../s/security.md) or [commodity](../c/commodity.md) on which the [options](../o/options.md) are based. This could be a stock, an [index](../i/index_instrument.md), a [currency](../c/currency.md) pair, or any other tradeable instrument.
2. **[Call Option](../c/call_option.md)**: A financial contract that gives the buyer the right, but not the obligation, to purchase the [underlying asset](../u/underlying_asset.md) at a predefined [strike price](../s/strike_price.md) before or at the [expiration date](../e/expiration_date.md).
3. **[Put Option](../p/put.md)**: A financial contract that gives the buyer the right, but not the obligation, to sell the [underlying asset](../u/underlying_asset.md) at a predefined [strike price](../s/strike_price.md) before or at the [expiration date](../e/expiration_date.md).
4. **[Strike Price](../s/strike_price.md)**: The fixed price at which the call and [put options](../p/put_options.md) can be exercised.
5. **[Expiration Date](../e/expiration_date.md)**: The specific date on which the [options contract](../o/options_contract.md) expires.

## How Short Straddle Works

In implementing a short [straddle](../s/straddle.md), the [trader](../t/trader.md) sells a [call option](../c/call_option.md) and a [put option](../p/put.md) with the same [strike price](../s/strike_price.md) and [expiration date](../e/expiration_date.md). The objective is to [profit](../p/profit.md) from the premiums collected from selling both [options](../o/options.md), under the assumption that the [underlying asset](../u/underlying_asset.md)'s price [will](../w/will.md) remain relatively stable. Here’s how it works in practice:

### Writing (Selling) the Options

- **Sell [Call Option](../c/call_option.md)**: By selling the [call option](../c/call_option.md), the [trader](../t/trader.md) promises to sell the [underlying asset](../u/underlying_asset.md) at the [strike price](../s/strike_price.md) if the option is exercised.
- **Sell [Put Option](../p/put.md)**: By selling the [put option](../p/put.md), the [trader](../t/trader.md) promises to buy the [underlying asset](../u/underlying_asset.md) at the [strike price](../s/strike_price.md) if the option is exercised.

### Premiums Collected

The seller ([writer](../w/writer.md)) collects premiums from both the call and [put options](../p/put_options.md), which is the compensation for the obligation taken to buy or sell the [asset](../a/asset.md) under the terms specified.

### Market Movement

- **Stable [Market](../m/market.md)**: If the price of the [underlying asset](../u/underlying_asset.md) remains close to the [strike price](../s/strike_price.md) until expiration, both the call and [put options](../p/put_options.md) are likely to expire worthless. The [trader](../t/trader.md) retains the premiums as [profit](../p/profit.md).
- **Bullish Movement**: If the [asset](../a/asset.md) price rises significantly above the [strike price](../s/strike_price.md), the [call option](../c/call_option.md) [will](../w/will.md) be exercised. The [trader](../t/trader.md) [will](../w/will.md) need to sell the [asset](../a/asset.md) at a potentially lower price, leading to a loss.
- **Bearish Movement**: If the [asset](../a/asset.md) price falls significantly below the [strike price](../s/strike_price.md), the [put option](../p/put.md) [will](../w/will.md) be exercised. The [trader](../t/trader.md) [will](../w/will.md) need to buy the [asset](../a/asset.md) at a potentially higher price, leading to a loss.

## Profitability Analysis

A short [straddle](../s/straddle.md) is primarily a strategy to [capitalize](../c/capitalize.md) on [market](../m/market.md) stability. The [trader](../t/trader.md) aims to generate [profit](../p/profit.md) through the premiums collected, betting that the [market](../m/market.md) [will](../w/will.md) not show significant price [volatility](../v/volatility.md). Here’s the detailed profitability analysis:

### Maximum Profit

The maximum [profit](../p/profit.md) is limited to the sum of the premiums collected from selling the call and [put options](../p/put_options.md). This scenario occurs if the [underlying asset](../u/underlying_asset.md)'s price at expiration is exactly at the [strike price](../s/strike_price.md), rendering both [options](../o/options.md) worthless.

### Break-even Points

There are two break-even points for a short [straddle](../s/straddle.md):
1. **Upper Break-even**: [Strike Price](../s/strike_price.md) + [Net Premium](../n/net_premium.md) Collected
2. **Lower Break-even**: [Strike Price](../s/strike_price.md) - [Net Premium](../n/net_premium.md) Collected

### Maximum Loss

The maximum loss can be substantial and technically unlimited on the upper side if the price of the [underlying asset](../u/underlying_asset.md) continues to rise indefinitely. Conversely, on the lower side, the loss is limited by the fact that the [underlying asset](../u/underlying_asset.md) 가격 cannot fall below zero.

## Risk Considerations

The short [straddle](../s/straddle.md) strategy is inherently risky due to the unlimited potential for loss. Key risks include:

1. **[Volatility Risk](../v/volatility_risk.md)**: A sudden spike in [market](../m/market.md) [volatility](../v/volatility.md) can lead to significant losses as the price of the [underlying asset](../u/underlying_asset.md) moves far from the [strike price](../s/strike_price.md) in either direction.
2. **[Liquidity Risk](../l/liquidity_risk.md)**: If the [options](../o/options.md) [market](../m/market.md) or the [underlying asset](../u/underlying_asset.md) lacks [liquidity](../l/liquidity.md), it may be challenging to exit the position without incurring substantial losses.
3. **[Margin](../m/margin.md) Requirements**: Writing uncovered [options](../o/options.md) may require significant [margin](../m/margin.md), [tying](../t/tying.md) up [capital](../c/capital.md) that could be used elsewhere.

## Example Scenario

Assume you are trading a stock (XYZ) currently priced at $100. You decide to implement a short [straddle](../s/straddle.md) by selling a [call option](../c/call_option.md) and a [put option](../p/put.md), both with a [strike price](../s/strike_price.md) of $100 and expiring in one month. The [premium](../p/premium.md) for the [call option](../c/call_option.md) is $5, and the [premium](../p/premium.md) for the [put option](../p/put.md) is $4.

### Collected Premiums

- [Call Option](../c/call_option.md): $5
- [Put Option](../p/put.md): $4
- Total Premiums: $9

### Potential Outcomes

- **Stable Price**: If the stock price remains at $100, both [options](../o/options.md) expire worthless. You keep the $9 [premium](../p/premium.md).
- **Price Increase**: If the stock price rises to $110, the [call option](../c/call_option.md) [will](../w/will.md) be exercised, and you [will](../w/will.md) incur a $1 loss (($110 - $100) - $9 = $1).
- **Price Decrease**: If the stock price falls to $90, the [put option](../p/put.md) [will](../w/will.md) be exercised, and you [will](../w/will.md) incur a $1 loss (($100 - $90) - $9 = $1).

### Break-even Points

- **Upper Break-even**: $100 + $9 = $109
- **Lower Break-even**: $100 - $9 = $91

## Strategies to Mitigate Risks

To manage the risks associated with a short [straddle](../s/straddle.md), traders may employ several techniques:

1. **Hedging**: Adding other positions to [offset](../o/offset.md) potential losses, such as buying other [options](../o/options.md) or [futures contracts](../f/futures_contracts.md).
2. **[Position Sizing](../p/position_sizing.md)**: Limiting the size of the position to control the overall [risk](../r/risk.md) in the portfolio.
3. **[Stop-Loss Orders](../s/stop-loss_orders.md)**: Setting predefined levels at which to exit the position if the [market](../m/market.md) moves unfavorably.
